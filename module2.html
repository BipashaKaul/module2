States of matter

Solid

Solid matter is composed of tightly packed particles. A solid will retain its shape; the particles are not free to move around

Liquid

The liquid state of matter is an intermediate phase between solid and gas. ... Liquids will flow and fill the lowest portion of a container, taking on the shape of the container but not changing in volume.

Gas

Gas is a state of matter that has no fixed shape and no fixed volume. Gases have lower density than other states of matter, such as solids and liquids.
